Research Summary
 
Geometry Processing and Modeling
My research mainly focuses on geometry processing and modeling, which is fundamental to most computer graphics applications. During the last decade, digital geometry processing (DGP) has received extensive research from the graphics community. Effective methods and tools have been developed for various discreet geometry problems including meshing, parameterization, discreet differential geometry, etc.
   Recently, more attention has been paid to high level geometry processing, which seeks to utilize high level (or semantic) shape information, such as symmetric structure, to facilitate more powerful shape analysis. Especially, man-made objects are of the most interest because they contain rich semantics yet are difficult to analyze due to significant shape variation. Currently, analyze-and-process is the most adopted paradigm for high level shape processing.
   My recent research interest is mainly on this emerging field - high level geometry processing. Currently, we are looking for effective means to co-analyze a set of 3D models of man-made objects (usually of the same class). Co-analysis is well motivated. For example, meaningful part correspondence between shapes in the set naturally reveals the semantics of the shape class.
